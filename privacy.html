Privacy

Last updated: June 20, 2026

What information does the app use?

  • App usage and foreground-app activity.
  • Your selected limits, blocked apps, and settings.
  • Messages submitted during an appeal.
  • Basic app interaction events through Firebase Analytics.

Why is this information used?

  • To measure and enforce screen-time limits.
  • To display blocking screens and notifications.
  • To review appeals and decide whether to grant more time.
  • To understand app performance and feature usage.

Where is information stored?

  • Limits, settings, app selections, and chat history are stored on your device.
  • Appeal messages are sent to The Judge server and processed by OpenAI.
  • Analytics events may be processed by Google Firebase.

Is information sold?

  • No personal information is sold.
  • No information is used for targeted advertising.

What permissions are required?

  • Usage access to identify limited apps and track screen time.
  • Overlay access to cover an app after its limit is reached.
  • Notifications to keep the screen-time service visible and active.
  • Internet access to submit appeals and record analytics.

How can information be removed?

  • Clear the app's storage or uninstall it to remove local information.
  • Contact us with questions about information processed off-device.

How can you contact us?

  • Email support@thejudgescreentime.com.
